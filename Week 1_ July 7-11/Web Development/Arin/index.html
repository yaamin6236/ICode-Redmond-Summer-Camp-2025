Roblox

Part 1: What is Roblox?

Roblox is an online platform that allows users to create games, or "experiences" for others to play. These immersive 3D experiences are created through Roblox's proprietary game engine, called Roblox Studio. It has an online currency called Robux which is used for in-game purchases.

Part 2: How it works

When you choose a game to play on your device (client), it connects to Roblox (server) and tells it the ID of the game that you requested. The server replies with the experience data, and the place loads on the client.

Part 3: History of Roblox

Roblox was created by David Baszucki alongside Erik Cassel in 2004 and publicly released in 2006. It was originally called "DynaBlocks" because of the default Stud and Inlet building block's resemblance to LEGO Bricks.

Part 4: Roblox Studio

As mentioned in part 1, Roblox Studio is Roblox's proprietary experience creation engine. You can build maps, and script it to your liking in Lua. Studio also supports user created Plugins that help you level up the funtionality and effiency of creating experiences.
