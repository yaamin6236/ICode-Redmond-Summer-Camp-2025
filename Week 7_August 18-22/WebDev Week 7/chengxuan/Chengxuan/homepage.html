Mount Everest

Location

Mount Everest is located on the border of China and Nepal. It is a part of the himlayas.

Facts

Height: Mount Everest stands at 8,848.86 meters (29,031.7 feet) above sea level (confirmed by China and Nepal in 2020). Location: It sits on the border between Nepal and the Tibet Autonomous Region of China. First Ascent: Sir Edmund Hillary (New Zealand) and Tenzing Norgay (Nepal) were the first confirmed people to reach the summit on May 29, 1953. Nepali Name: In Nepal, Everest is called Sagarmatha, meaning “Forehead of the Sky.” Tibetan Name: In Tibet, it is called Chomolungma, meaning “Goddess Mother of the World.”

Dangers

Here is a list of dangers in Mount Everest
    • 1. Altitude sickness When you get to the death zone in Mount Everest, the lack of air can result in brain swelling and fluid in lungs
        2. Extreme weather when on the mountain, temperatures can drop below -40°F, and blizzards can appear with no warning.
          3. Falls There are ice covered slopes on Mount Everest, so there are hazards of slipping and causing harm
            4. Limited rescue operations It is very hard for helicopters to rescue you, and the only thing that can save you us yourself or other hikers.

            Equipment

            specialized Gear: You will need a full range of high-altitude mountaineering equipment, including: Clothing Layers: Base layers, mid-layers, down suit, insulated jackets and pants, waterproof/windproof shell layers. Footwear: Mountaineering boots, trekking boots/shoes, camp booties. Handwear: Insulated gloves, liner gloves, mittens. Headwear & Eyewear: Climbing helmet, balaclava, glacier glasses, goggles, sun hat, wool/synthetic hat. Sleeping Gear: Expedition-quality sleeping bags (rated to at least -40°F), sleeping pads, camp booties. Technical Gear: Crampons, ice axe, harness, carabiners, belay device, ascenders, rappel device, accessory cord, trekking poles. Safety Equipment: Avalanche probe, avalanche airbag, emergency bivvy. Navigation: Navigation device, headlamp with extra batteries. Personal Items: First aid kit, medications (including Diamox for altitude sickness if needed), toiletries, water bottles, thermos. Oxygen: Most climbers utilize supplemental oxygen, which includes cylinders, a mask, and a regulator.
