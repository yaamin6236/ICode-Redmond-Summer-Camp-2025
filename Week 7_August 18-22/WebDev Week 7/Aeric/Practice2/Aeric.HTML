Vietnam

Vietnam is a Southeast Asian country located on the eastern edge of the Indochinese Peninsula. It shares land borders with China to the north, Laos and Cambodia to the west, and has a long eastern coastline along the South China Sea. The country stretches over 1,000 miles from north to south, with a diverse landscape that includes mountains, forests, river deltas, and beaches. Its capital is Hanoi, located in the north, while Ho Chi Minh City (formerly Saigon) is the largest city in the south.

Major cities

Some of the major cities include Ho Chi Minh, Hanoi, Da Nang, and Can Tho.

Hanoi – The Capital City

Hanoi, located in northern Vietnam, is the country's capital and one of the oldest cities in Southeast Asia. With a rich history spanning over a thousand years, it's known for its centuries-old architecture, historical temples, and French colonial buildings. The city's cultural heart lies in the Old Quarter, a maze of narrow streets filled with street food, local markets, and traditional shops. Hanoi is also home to significant landmarks like the Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum, One Pillar Pagoda, and Hoan Kiem Lake. The city has a more traditional, slower-paced vibe compared to its southern counterpart.

Ho Chi Minh City (Saigon) – The Economic Hub

Formerly known as Saigon, Ho Chi Minh City is the largest city in Vietnam and serves as the country’s economic engine. Located in the south, it’s a bustling metropolis with a dynamic blend of modern skyscrapers, historic French colonial buildings, and vibrant street life. District 1 is the commercial and cultural center, home to landmarks like the Notre-Dame Cathedral, Ben Thanh Market, and War Remnants Museum. HCMC is known for its fast pace, thriving business environment, and vibrant nightlife, making it a magnet for young professionals and tourists alike.

Da Nang – The Coastal Gateway

Da Nang, located in central Vietnam, is a rapidly developing coastal city known for its clean beaches, modern infrastructure, and laid-back lifestyle. It serves as a gateway to famous UNESCO World Heritage Sites like Hoi An, My Son Sanctuary, and the Imperial City of Hue. The city itself features attractions like the Dragon Bridge, Marble Mountains, and Ba Na Hills (home to the iconic Golden Bridge held by giant hands). Da Nang strikes a balance between urban living and natural beauty, with a growing reputation as a livable city.

Can Tho – The Heart of the Mekong Delta

Can Tho is the largest city in the Mekong Delta, located in southern Vietnam. Known for its floating markets (especially Cai Rang), lush canals, and fertile farmland, the city plays a crucial role in Vietnam’s agricultural output. Life here is slower and deeply tied to the rhythms of the river. Can Tho offers a more relaxed, authentic experience of delta culture with attractions like the Binh Thuy Ancient House, Ninh Kieu Wharf, and various river cruises through rural villages and mangrove forests.
